For our HCI course we had two dedicated weeks to come-up with something in the realm of AR & VR. The idea was to create something ‚Äúinnovative‚Äù, creative and with the new skills we just acquired. There was also an emphasis on cross branching with various technologies forms.

Day 1

Day 1 was an interesting day for me, I was not initially set on doing something in the AR & VR. So, my day started off with researching what platforms we can use but before we could do that we needed to know if we doing AR or VR. We ultimately choose to with AR. via GIPHY



Unreal - was to Graphically dependent and with only having integrated Graphics, I knew I would be spending more time waiting for my program to Respond then actually tinkering. Three,js was an interesting platform it offered a wide range of possibilities, there was extensive examples of AR/VR and what you could do with it. There was a problem though and that was that we would be dependent on code and our abilities in it. This was a con for us so we looked into Unity. Unity provided a tool built in tools from Google with the AR Foundation. Another plus was that Unity was not as graphically intense as Unreal allowing for us to have smoother workflow.

Once we understood our platform we looked further into what tools where available inside of Unity. We saw that there was a Vuforia engine that could detect images and various objects and had the features that was missing in AR Foundation. But we decided to go with what unity provided through their AR project template which was based on the XR-Plugin and AR Foundation.

Final Project


Our Final project trip town is an imaginary town with various interactive elements. We would have hoped to do more and expand the world more. Because the way it stands at the moment its very much a novelty. I play with it once or twice but then I am done with it.

Our world has multiple 3D assets that have been put together to build a lifelike town. We also have various lighting elements involved. For example the fire reflects its lights point of surrounding areas, to create the illusion of heat. The weather is controlled by a weather pack that has very environments inside of it. The api is called through an external plug-in called real-time weather. The clouds are downloaded from the Unity asset store but they are connected to the weather. The cars are stationary but we did try and animate a two cars, the animations are not very smooth however more work would need to be put into these. We also added a gaze interaction that cause information to popup on certain building. In our town the popup are located at the gas station‚Äôs indicating you the fuel prices. The gaze interaction is 3 part setup, one script is used for the camera which notifies the system that the camera is looking at you and once it reaches your hit box you need to show yourself. The second part is the actual transformation of the board coming up from the building. The 3rd script is the script that tells the board to rotate in the direction the camera is facing. The last element of our trip town is the ability to freely move the town in the xyz axis. On top of that you can scale and rotate respectively.
